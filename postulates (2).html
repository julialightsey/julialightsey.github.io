Although most people haven't formulated their thoughts regarding design into specific "laws" as I have here, I believe that all experienced engineers have at least unwritten laws or rules through which they self govern their designs. The laws as I've stated them here are based on a three decade career as an engineer and developer as well as almost two decades of development on SQL Server and with set based languages in general.

A human being should be able to change a diaper, plan an invasion, butcher a hog, conn a ship, design a building, write a sonnet, balance accounts, build a wall, set a bone, comfort the dying, take orders, give orders, cooperate, act alone, solve equations, analyze a new problem, pitch manure, program a computer, cook a tasty meal, fight efficiently, die gallantly. If you disagree with one or all; good. Just make sure you are clear on why you disagree and what your opposing or preferred stance is. Disagreement itself is rarely useful just as restatement of problems is rarely welcome. Presentation of an alternative or a solution is where you begin to become useful to others. I've presented my positions here as "strawman proposals" if not more.

  1. [1st_law.clarity] - Well written software can be re-factored, poorly written software can only be tolerated.
  2. [2nd_law.robustness] - There is no such thing as "bad" data.
  3. [3rd_law.complexity] - If a method is too complex to effectively unit test, it is too complex.
  4. [4th_law.objectives] - If you have no objectives, you will never meet them; if you have no principles, it won't matter if you do.
  5. [5th_law.normalization] - Normalization is not the normal state of a design, but it is the preferred state.
  6. [6th_law.self_documenting] - Good software should be self-documenting.
  7. [7th_law.testing] - If it can't be measured, it doesn't exist.
  8. [8th_law.elegance] - Complexity must justify itself.
  9. [9th_law.innovation] - When dogma becomes an impediment to the achievement of your objectives, perhaps it is time to shoot your dog.
  10. [10th_law.purpose] - You can't miss fast enough to win a gunfight.
